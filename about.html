WikiPOD

About WikiPOD

WikiPOD transforms digital knowledge into tangible books. Users select articles from Wikipedia, and WikiPOD's layout engine optimizes the content for printing. The result is then available for purchase as a paperback book via print-on-demand technology.

Why Wikipedia?

Wikipedia is the largest online encyclopedia, offering an extensive collection of articles covering a wide range of topics contributed by volunteers from around the world. Contrary to popular belief, most articles are reliable and well-researched thanks to Wikipedia's editorial standards and rigorous review process. Wikipedia's licensing policy allows content to be freely copied, modified, and redistributed. This makes it perfect source material for books made with WikiPOD.

Why Books?

Some people prefer tangible reference materials or simply appreciate the value of a physical book. Some practical examples include travelers creating custom travel guides, students annotating physical copies, and language learners practicing with text on any topic.

Features and Limitations

WikiPOD can create books from 4 to 800 pages, always printed in black and white at A5 size (5.83 x 8.27 in). Books under 32 pages are printed as saddleback (where pages are stapled instead of glued), which is typically a bit more expensive. For the best value, it's encouraged to combine multiple articles into a single book.

WikiPOD attempts to optimize the print layout, but some articles contain content with unique considerations. Before ordering a printed book, be sure to check the provided preview for any formatting issues.

WikiPOD allows users to toggle the visibility of images, tables, and infoboxes (the sidebar summary that appears with most Wikipedia articles), which are all hidden by default. These elements can be especially difficult to format for print. Hiding them also reduces the page count and cost of printing.

WikiPOD supports content in any language. Simply paste in the Wikipedia URL, or add a language code in brackets before the article title. For example, "[fr] Tour Eiffel" retrieves the French article about the Eiffel Tower.
